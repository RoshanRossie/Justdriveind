how it works

We Mostly Work On sustainable Waste solution that produces ethanol from garbage. Over 2 billion tons of municipal solid waste are generated globally every year, and this number is expected to double by 2050. Food waste alone accounts for one-third of all food produced, with developing countries losing up to 50% of their crops to post-harvest losses and waste. E-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream globally, increasing by 20% annually, posing challenges due to its complex components and toxic materials.

Understanding the diverse drivers of waste generation is crucial for designing effective solutions. By addressing consumption patterns, promoting sustainable choices, and investing in innovative technologies, we can move towards a future where waste is minimized, resources are valued, and responsible management becomes the norm.

Remember, waste generation is not just a technical issue; it's a reflection of our relationship with the environment and our consumption habits. Let's take ownership of the waste we generate and actively seek ways to reduce, reuse, and recycle, paving the way for a more sustainable future for all.
